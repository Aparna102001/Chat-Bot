Resources

Storing water in a cool, dark place helps prevent contamination and algae growth. This is essential for maintaining water quality during emergencies, ensuring that the stored water remains safe for consumption.

Seal Containers

Use airtight containers to store water. This prevents contaminants from entering and ensures the water remains safe for consumption over longer periods. Regularly check and maintain the containers to prevent leaks and contamination.

Label and Date Containers

Clearly label and date water containers. This helps track the storage duration and ensures water is rotated and used within safe time frames. It's important to replace stored water every six months to maintain its quality.

Emergency Water Sources

During emergencies, safe water sources include bottled water, water boiled for at least one minute, and water treated with disinfectants such as chlorine or iodine. It's crucial to avoid using water that may be contaminated unless it has been properly treated to ensure safety.

Emergency Planning

Developing an emergency plan that includes water storage and sanitation measures is vital. Regularly review and update the plan to ensure readiness. This includes having a supply of clean water, knowing how to treat water, and understanding essential sanitation practices to protect health during emergencies.
